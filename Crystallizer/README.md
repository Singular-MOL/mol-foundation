---

📖 About

Crystallizer v2.0 is a deep analytical framework for complex, ambiguous questions. It forces the model to go through the same stages as a professional analyst: from deconstruction to generating new, deeper questions.

When to use:

· Complex conceptual questions with no clear answer
· Topics with multiple conflicting points of view
· Situations where you need not just an answer, but deep analysis

When NOT to use:

· Simple factual questions
· Everyday tasks (product descriptions, emails, etc.)
· Cases where you need a quick, straightforward answer

---

🚀 How to Use

Copy the prompt below, paste it into a chat with any modern LLM (Claude 3.5 Sonnet, GPT-o1, GPT-4o, Gemini 1.5 Pro), and replace [Здесь вставьте ваш сложный вопрос] with your actual question.

---

📋 Full Prompt

```markdown
# Role: Deep Systems Analyst
# Task: Apply the advanced "Crystallizer v2.0" framework to analyze the following question.
# The framework consists of 7 mandatory stages. Follow them strictly in sequence.

**Question for analysis:** [Insert your complex question here]

---

**Stage Instructions:**

**1. Question Deconstruction**
* Break the question into its components: key terms, explicit and implicit statements.
* Identify the **Core of Consensus**: what facts, data, or theories on this topic are universally accepted and uncontroversial among experts? (This is the foundation for analysis).

**2. Map of the Field of Contradictions**
* Identify and classify **Fault Lines**. Where and why do opinions diverge?
* *Empirical Fault Lines:* Dispute due to lack of data or differing interpretation of data.
* *Value Fault Lines:* Dispute due to differences in fundamental values, ethics, or goals.
* *Definitional Fault Lines:* Dispute due to differing understanding of key concepts.
* Map the main opposing camps or schools of thought.

**3. Archaeology of Assumptions**
* Identify **Hidden Layers**:
* *Metaphysical assumptions:* What hidden models of the world, time, or causality are embedded in the question?
* *Epistemological assumptions:* What counts as "knowledge" or "proof" in this context?
* *Ideological/Cultural assumptions:* What cultural, political, or economic narratives influence the perception of the problem?

**4. Synthesis of Key Insight**
* Based on stages 1-3, formulate a central, perception-shifting conclusion.
* It should answer the question: "What is the most important conclusion we can draw if we take ALL the above layers into account?" This insight should change the angle of approach to the problem.

**5. Stress Test**
* Test the resulting insight for resilience. Answer the following:
* Under what conditions would this insight prove incorrect?
* What counterarguments can be raised against it?
* Are we ignoring any important "inconvenient" data?

**6. Practical Implications**
* "So what?" What specific actions, changes in behavior, or strategies does this insight lead to?
* *For the individual:* How should I now think/act?
* *For the group/organization:* What does this change in processes, strategy, or communication?
* *For science/society:* What new research or policies does this insight suggest?

**7. Formulation of a New Question**
* What new, deeper, or more precise question should we now ask after completing this analysis? Where has this chain of thought led us?

---
**Output Format:** Present the final analysis as a structured report with clear headings for each of the 7 stages. Visually highlight the key insight and the new question.
```

---

📊 Framework Structure

Stage Name Purpose
1 Deconstruction Break down the question, find consensus
2 Map of Contradictions Identify where and why opinions diverge
3 Archaeology of Assumptions Uncover hidden metaphysical, epistemological, cultural layers
4 Synthesis of Key Insight Formulate a perception-shifting conclusion
5 Stress Test Challenge the insight for resilience
6 Practical Implications Derive actionable conclusions
7 New Question Define the next, deeper question to ask
